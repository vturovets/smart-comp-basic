The analysis compares two datasets from IT system performance evaluations, focusing on the 95th percentiles (P95) of response times. The second dataset, from "input-Apr16-17-2025_2.csv," has a lower mean of 175.00 and a skewness of 3.08, also indicating a right-skewed distribution. Both datasets show evidence of unimodality, but the second dataset has a higher bimodality coefficient, suggesting a potential for multiple modes.

The comparison of the 95th percentiles shows a statistically significant difference between the two datasets. Dataset 1 has a P95 of 997.58, while Dataset 2 has a P95 of 563.62. The confidence intervals for P95 are [944.49, 1063.66] for Dataset 1 and [541.00, 599.20] for Dataset 2, with margins of error of 5.97 and 5.16, respectively.
